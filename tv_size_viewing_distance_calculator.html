The 4 golden principles
30° viewing angle
The human eye comfortably takes in a 30° field of view. This is the basis of the SMPTE standard — the source of the ÷1.6 formula used by interior designers worldwide.
Eye-level placement
The center of the TV should sit at seated eye level — typically 42–48 inches from the floor. Most people hang TV far too high, causing neck strain and ruining the room's proportion.
Room proportion rule
TV width should not exceed 2/3 of the furniture it sits on or the wall section it occupies. A TV that dominates the wall kills the balance of the entire room.
Resolution adjustment
4K TVs allow you to sit 1.5× closer than older 1080p screens without seeing pixels. This means a 4K TV can be larger for the same room — you can step up one size category safely.
